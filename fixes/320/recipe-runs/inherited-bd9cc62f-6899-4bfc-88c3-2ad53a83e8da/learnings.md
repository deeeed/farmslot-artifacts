# Learnings ā€” PR #320 follow-up (update-branch rename review fixes)

- The blocking defect was a PR-boundedness gap: `update-branch` was added to `PR_BOUND_FLOW_TYPES` but the chain builder's ticketā†’PR-ref conversion still hard-coded `flowType === 'pr-complete'`. Gating on `PR_BOUND_FLOW_TYPES.has(flowType)` (not a second `||` compare) keeps a future PR-bound chain flow correct by construction.
- "No production caller" nits have two valid resolutions ā€” wire or drop. Here the force-pushā†’merge downgrade needed an `allowForcePush` signal that exists nowhere (the worker resolves at runtime), so adding one would be a speculative feature. Dropping `resolveBranchUpdateStrategy` and giving `isBranchUpdateStrategy` a real caller in the writer was the honest minimal fix.
- The real source of truth for a flow's required outcome artifact is `worker-terminal-contract.cjs` (`report.md` for update-branch), NOT `FLOW_WORKER_REPORT_ARTIFACTS` in protocol. Three surfaces (contract .cjs, protocol constant, docs) had drifted to three different names ā€” always trace the enforcing tool before picking the canonical name.
- "Diff noise" nits aren't always safe to revert: the `slotRelease` single-line form is 113 chars and fails `prettier --check` on `main`, so the PR's wrapping is Prettier-mandated. Verified by running prettier against `git show main:<file>` before deciding ā€” waived the nit with evidence instead of reintroducing a lint failure.
- Markdown table edits shift Prettier's column-width reflow: shortening one cell forced a whole-table re-pad. Run `prettier --write` on touched `.md` tables (they were clean on main) rather than hand-aligning pipes.
